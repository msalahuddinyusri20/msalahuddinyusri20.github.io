## Kadet Remaja Sekolah (KRS) — Foot Drill & Leadership
**2021 – 2025 | School, State & National Level**

Of all my extracurricular commitments, KRS represents the longest and most 
demanding. What began as a junior marcher in Form 2 grew into a four-year 
journey of discipline, precision, and eventually, the responsibility of 
training others.

- Competed in foot drill competitions at **school and state level** across 
  multiple years, representing SMK Agama Putrajaya in high-pressure 
  competitive environments.
- Selected to march in the **National Day Parade for 2 consecutive years 
  (2022 & 2023)** — a nationally televised event broadcast live, requiring 
  a standard of precision that only the most disciplined cadets are chosen for.
- At Form 4, **collaborated across uniform bodies with the Scouts team** 
  for a national-level formation march competition due to insufficient 
  participants. Trained together from zero over **2 months of nightly 
  sessions**, achieving competition-ready precision and contributing to a 
  **4th place finish at State Level** in the same year.
- In senior years, took on the role of **training junior cadets** in marching 
  techniques, transferring precision skills, correcting form, and building 
  collective discipline within the team until competition standard was achieved.

*The discipline of drill where a single misaligned step costs the entire 
team is not different from engineering. Precision is not optional. Neither is the 
responsibility to bring others up to your standard.*

## Volleyball — From Zero to State Level
**2021 – 2025 | School & State Level (MSSWP)**

Few things on this portfolio reflect personal growth as honestly as volleyball. 
I entered secondary school unable to perform basic techniques. By Form 4, I 
was competing at state level as a skilled middle blocker.

- Joined the school volleyball team after the coach identified potential
  with near-zero prior experience.
- Developed specialisation as a **middle blocker**, a role demanding rapid 
  reading of opponent attack patterns and split-second reactive precision.
- Progressed through 2 years of competitive play: State participant MSSWP (2024), **4th Place,
  MSSWP U18 State Championship (2025)**.
- Played within a team built on mutual respect and shared development contributing to a
  cohesive team culture and collaborative team tactics that performed consistently.

*Volleyball taught me that coachability the willingness to be corrected 
and improved is what separates someone with potential from someone who 
actually delivers.*

## EXCO Academic — Pengawas Prep Programme
**2024 – 2025 | Prefectorial Board, SMK Agama Putrajaya**

As the Academic Executive Council member for the Prefectorial Board, I 
identified a recurring problem in our school's hostel prep sessions — 
students were consistently unproductive during designated study hours, 
choosing to sleep or play instead of completing assignments.

Rather than relying entirely on enforcement, I worked with the board to 
design and implement the **Pengawas Prep Programme**, a structured 
prefect-led supervision system for night prep classrooms.

- Diagnosed the root cause: absence of consistent, accountable supervision 
  during prep hours.
- Designed a rotation schedule assigning trained prefects to monitor and 
  maintain discipline in prep classrooms each night.
- Managed a team of 8 Academic Committee Members in executing the programme 
  consistently across the school term.
- Maintained physical study environments including the hostel studying room, 
  ensuring a clean and conducive studying environment for peak academic performance.

*This was my first real experience of identifying a systemic problem, 
designing a solution, and managing a team to implement it, a process 
I intend to apply at a far larger scale as an engineer.*

## School Holiday Facilitator Programme
**2023 | Primary School Educational Camp**

During a school break in 2023, I volunteered as a facilitator for a 
holiday programme serving primary school children, conducting educational 
activities and managing group sessions.

- Planned and facilitated structured educational activities for young 
  children across multiple sessions.
- Managed group dynamics and behaviour in a high-energy, unpredictable 
  environment requiring constant patience, adaptability and calmness.

*Working with young children taught me something no leadership role in 
school had which was regulating my own emotions under genuine frustration. 
The ability to remain composed when things don't go to plan is
one of the most underrated professional skills there is.*
